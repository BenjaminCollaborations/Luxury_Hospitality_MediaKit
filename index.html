About

Ben Arnst

Photographer · Filmmaker · Director · Producer

Emmy Award–winning cinematographer for National Geographic's Life Below Zero, with 100+ days filming in Alaska hunting and trapping with native Inuit and Iñupiaq communities. Ben began as a "physical architect," building the world's largest architectural composites and sculptural builds — most notably the iconic façade of the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art — before evolving into a "digital architect" who builds tech-enabled real estate and brand storytelling systems.

In real estate, Ben's work sits at the intersection of marketing, partnerships, and trust: helping projects earn attention, credibility, and demand through world-class media, narrative strategy, and distribution — supporting capital formation and accelerating go-to-market.
